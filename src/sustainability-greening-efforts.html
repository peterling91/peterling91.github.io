Inspiring sustainability and community engagement

Our Green Campus

Located at the heart of Ang Mo Kio, our campus showcases well-planned green spaces amidst training facilities and staff offices. The landscaping incudes beautiful gardens with interaction spaces, koi ponds, trees and shrubs.

Using the concept of biophilic design, we also plant sustainable plant species across common linkways in campus. The plants provided additional shade and help to keep out rain.

What we've done

Towards efficient campus operations

We look into improving our campus operations in these areas.

Energy efficiency

To cut down on energy, we have initiatives such as water leak detections and motion-sensors lighting in linkways

In addition, we have upgraded our chillers with optimised cooling towers.

Green building and infrastructure

We've implemented digital twins in several buildings, creating virtual replicas that collect and analyze real-time data to simulate and optimize smart building outcomes.

Water conservation

We have built rainwater collection stations at various campus buildings. The water collected is used to irrigate plants in the campus.

Sustainable transportation

To encourage our NYP community to consider using electric vehicles (EV) options, we have installed three EV charging points in campus. We are also in the development of new cycling lanes.

Sustainable food practices

We promote sustainable food choices in campus. For example, L'Rez - NYP's student-run restaurant - works with industry partner Unilever to promote plant-based options in its menus

Our highlights

Green building and infrastructure

Innovative and Sustainable Architectural Design

Our approach to green building begins with innovative architectural design that prioritizes sustainability at every stage. NYP’s buildings are constructed with a focus on minimizing environmental impact through the use of sustainable materials, energy-efficient systems, and design elements that maximize natural light and ventilation.

These elements not only reduce energy consumption but also enhance the well-being of our students and staff.

High-performance Building Envelopes

Our buildings are designed with advanced insulation and energy-efficient windows to reduce the need for heating and cooling, significantly cutting energy consumption.

Green Roofs and Vertical Gardens

These features not only add aesthetic value but also improve air quality, reduce urban heat island effects, and provide insulation for the buildings beneath them.

Natural Lighting and Ventilation

Large windows and strategically placed skylights allow for maximum natural light, reducing the need for artificial lighting. Cross-ventilation designs help maintain comfortable indoor air quality and temperature without excessive reliance on air conditioning.
