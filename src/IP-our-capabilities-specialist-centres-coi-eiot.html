Centre of Innovation for Electronics & Internet of Things (COI-EIoT)

About centre

Powered by a deep expertise in electronics and computer engineering, the Centre of Innovation for Electronics & Internet of Things (COI-EIoT) was jointly established by NYP and Enterprise Singapore (formerly SPRING Singapore) in 2008. Harnessing the advent of new-generation systems and technologies like Internet of Things, autonomous systems, artificial intelligence and analytics, the COI-EIoT co-creates with industry end-to-end solutions in areas as diverse as transportation and mobility, healthcare, retail and smart manufacturing.

Our capabilities

Innovation & development support

Design consultancy and development of products/processes/services leveraging on embedded technology, wireless communications, imaging technology and infocomm technologies etc.

Technology transfer

Technology and knowledge transfer including IP creation and translation to marketplace.

Product testing

Services including product/ system characterization & performance measurements, pre-compliance & verification, test engineering etc. In addition, we also do test-bedding, user-trials and test marketing of new solutions.

Market research and intelligence

We offer key insights on market trends and emerging tech. With precise analysis, we can help aid businesses in informed decisions, innovation, and competitive edge for growth.

Antenna test & measurement an-echoic chamber

The Antenna Test & Measurement An-echoic Chamber is a cutting-edge facility designed for the precise evaluation and analysis of antenna performance. This chamber serves as a controlled environment, free from external interference, allowing for accurate testing of antennas across various frequencies. Its specialized design, featuring anechoic materials on the walls, floor, and ceiling, eliminates reflections and echoes, providing a near-perfect radio frequency (RF) testing environment. Researchers and engineers utilize this facility to conduct comprehensive tests, measure antenna characteristics, assess radiation patterns, and optimize antenna designs for optimal performance in real-world applications. The chamber plays a crucial role in ensuring the reliability and efficiency of antennas across diverse industries such as telecommunications, aerospace, and wireless technology.
