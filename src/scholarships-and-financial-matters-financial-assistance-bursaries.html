Can I apply?

Government and/or private funded bursaries are available to the following groups of students:

  • Full-time diploma & PFP students
  • Part-time diploma students

(The information above is effective from AY2023/24)

Government-funded bursaries for full-time diploma & PFP Students (Singapore Citizen)

  • The type of government bursary that you will be awarded will depend on whether you have met the corresponding income eligibility of the bursary award. You therefore need only to make one application for the different government bursaries – HECB, HEB or DFP Bursary.
  • As the majority of full-time subsidised students are eligible for government bursaries, you are highly encouraged to apply for government bursaries early in March/April so that your application can be approved in time for settlement of fees as well as be considered for additional selected private funded bursaries, awards and grants that are given out early in Semester 1. There is no need for separate and additional application for these private funded bursaries, awards and grants.
  • Student must not be a recipient of full scholarship (cover tuition fees and allowance)/sponsorship in the same academic year.
  • Student on Mendaki Tertiary Tuition Fee Subsidy scheme is eligible to apply for bursary.

Private funded bursaries, awards and grants (Singaporean Citizen & Singapore PR)

  • With generous donations from industry partners, charitable organisations and philanthropic individuals, NYP is able to provide financial assistance to needy students in the form of bursaries, awards & grants. Click here to view our donors list.
  • For Singaporean full-time subsidised students, once you apply for government bursary in an academic year, you may then be considered by NYP for private funded bursaries, awards and grants, if you meet the donor’s requirement including income eligibility. You are not required to separately apply for private-funded bursaries, awards and grants in that academic year.
  • For Singapore Permanent Residents students, you are encouraged to apply for private donor bursaries, awards and grants during the stipulated application period as shown in the table above.
  • Student must not be a recipient of full scholarship/sponsorship in the same academic year

How do I apply?

Step 1

Apply online

  • Apply online through NYP Student Portal during the application period.
  • Click “Apply Financial Assistance and Awards” -> “Bursaries”.
  • Click “Confirm” to submit your bursary application.
  • Check the acknowledgement email sent to your NYP email account if you have successfully completed application. You must upload all required supporting documents within 5 days from the date of application (using the link in the acknowledgment email or via File Upload System under Step 3).
  • Remain contactable via NYP email or mobile phone after application and document submission. NYP bursary team will contact you if further clarifications are required.
  • Download System user guide (1.8MB) and FAQ on online application.
Apply online now
Step 2

Gather supporting documents

  • Gather documents as indicated in the Checklist of supporting documents (0.7MB).
  • Scan softcopies of documents using Microsoft Office Lens (Android/IOS) if you do not have a scanner at home.
  • Download Checklist of supporting documents (0.7MB) and FAQ.
Step 3

Upload supporting documents

  • Upload supporting documents using the link provided in email (see step 1d) or file upload system link.
  • Complete upload of supporting documents within 5 days after completion of application in Step 1.
  • Download upload supporting documents user guide (1.8 MB) and FAQ.

Important information

  • Full-time diploma students who need financial assistance are encouraged to apply for bursary as early as possible. Some private donor bursaries will be available as a top up bursary in early semester 1 (Apr to Jun) and will be awarded to selected government bursary recipients.
  • Applicants are to submit only ONE application in any application period within the academic year and they are not required to specify the bursary type or bursary name in the application.
  • Applicants who are successful in their applications will receive only ONE bursary in the academic year, be it a government or private funded bursary.
  • Successful applicants must either have an existing GIRO arrangement with NYP (with “Approved” status) OR PayNow account (link to student’s NRIC) in order to receive the bursary disbursement.
  • Bursary award is tenable for one academic year only. Students will have to submit a new application if they wish to be considered for the bursary again in the subsequent academic year.
  • Students are to remain contactable via NYP email or mobile phone after submission. NYP bursary team will contact you if further clarifications are required.
