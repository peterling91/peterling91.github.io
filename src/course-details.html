Animation, Games & Visual Effects (C29)

View course modules
Scroll to explore

Join us on this exciting journey to create visual worlds!

Minimum entry requirements

4 - 10 points
3 - 9 points

Are you fascinated by games or movies that capture your attention with compelling digital characters, animation or visual effects?

Calling out to you, talented folks, who are...

creative. artistic. ingenious.

Here's what you’ll learn with us

  • Our comprehensive foundation modules will teach you broad skills in animation, games and visual effects to help you become tomorrow’s great media entertainers. Look forward to exciting careers with top animation and VFX companies such as Lucasfilm Animation and Industrial Light & Magic, or top game companies such as Ubisoft and Bandai Namco. Earn industry certifications from our partners like Epic Games in addition to your diploma.

    By the end of this diploma, you’ll gain the following course competencies:

    • 2D Art
    • 3D Art
    • Storytelling
    • Real-Time Technology
    • Animation or Games or Visual Effects

  • Learning Units

    • 22 Competency Units (CmU): Each CmU integrates skills and knowledge from different disciplines. This enables the students to fulfil specific work-tasks upon completion.
    • 4 Work-Integration-Units (WIU): A WIU is a workplace contextualised project. It allows students to tackle real-world work problems under the guidance of our educators, so that our students graduate ready for the workplace.

    1 Internship Programme (ITP)

    In Year 3, all students will have a chance to do a 12-week internship with our industry partners and gain hands-on experience to be confident in the workplace.

    The ITP is one of the WIUs.

    1 Final Year Project (FYP)

    In Year 3, all students will do a final-year project in their specialisation of choice. This is a final project to showcase the skills that they have gained in the diploma.

    The FYP is one of the WIUs.

    11 General Studies Modules (GSM)

    General Studies Modules allow you to explore other areas of interest beyond your chosen area of specialisation. You will have to take 6 prescribed GSMs and 5 elective GSMs across Year 1 and 2.

    3 Specialisations to choose from

    In Year 2, students will choose a specialisation, which will determine the Specialisation CmUs that you take in Year 2 and Year 3.

    You can choose 1 specialisation from the following:

    • Animation
    • Games
    • Visual Effects

Internships

Industry experience is essential. The School of Design & Media (SDM) believes in providing transferable skills that will prepare our graduates for work and life.

Pre-Visualisation ProductionWIU

Duration
60 hrs
Duration
60 hrs
Duration
60 hrs

Students will need to complete Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which can be in the form of an internship or project that mirror work situations where students can demonstrate their skills and competencies.

Pre-visualization is an essential stage in the digital media production as it provides a preview of the written content in visual form. In this WIU, learners will demonstrate their competency in creating an original content based on the concepts of storyboarding and the principles of animation that will provide industry realism in their pre-production planning. During this process, learners will also enhance their ability in communicating and collaborating in a team in order to pitch their ideas from conception to implementation.

Relevant competency units

This WIU integrates knowledge from the following competency units:

  • Storyboard & Animatics
  • Figure Drawing
  • 2D Animation
  • Digital Painting
  • 3D Workflow
